Upholding the woman's right across the globe

Background

Menstruation (also termed period or bleeding) is the process of a woman discharging (through the vagina) blood and other materials from the lining of the uterus at about one monthly interval from puberty until menopause (ceasing of regular menstrual cycles), except during pregnancy.  This discharging process lasts about 3-5 days. It is advisable to use a pad for 8 hours which means 4 pads are needed in a day and 12-20 pads are needed for a cycle.
It is inconveniencing for some Girls and Women that they require medication, oftentimes because they have little knowledge of Menstrual Hygiene Management. 
Menstrual hygiene management is described as the process whereby “women and adolescent girls use a clean menstrual hygiene management (MHM) material to absorb or collect blood that can be changed in privacy as often as necessary for the duration of the menstruation period, using soap and water for washing their bodies as required and having access to facilities to dispose of used menstrual management material” (UNICEF, WHO 2014). 
Not having access to menstrual management products and little knowledge on mensuration keeps women from participating in socio‐economic activities when on their periods, resulting in a loss of economic activity in their communities as well as the disenfranchisement of the women. Additionally, women often resort to alternatives such as mattress stuffing, leaves, cloth, rags and even mud etc., which are neither hygienic nor practical of which, these things can cause reproductive tract infections.
Girls and Women in these rural areas lack proper knowledge on handling menstruation due to lack of knowledge on sanitary products to use, do not have access to the conventional sanitary products, lack of water and method of disposal. Our goal is to educate these communities on methods of disposal, to engage and educate them on climate change, how to prevent climate hazards through proper sanitary disposal and Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH).
According to UNICEF, 1 in 10 girls in Sub‐Saharan Africa will miss school due to menstrual related issues. This has major ramifications on the likelihood of girls staying in education, which in turn has knock‐on effects on all aspects of girls’ lives. 
The United Nations Children’s Fund (UNICEF) in its 2015 study in three Nigerian states — Katsina, Anambra and Osun — revealed that menstruating school girls face great discomfort during their menstrual period. They often experience anxiety, abdominal pain and cramps, nausea and vomiting, dizziness, and a loss of appetite. As a result, many of the girls miss classes and even when they manage to attend, they are distracted.
In Nigeria where education is almost for free, 75% of the girls miss school during their monthly period; on average, they miss five days of school a month — about six weeks of learning in a session. Furthermore, research ﬁnding showed that girls' capacity to manage their periods is aﬀected by lack of access to aﬀordable hygienic sanitary materials disposal options for used materials, adequate water supply, clean toilets, hand washing facilities and access to changing rooms.  If these facilities are not always available in school, it exposes many girls to manage their periods with great discomfort and in unhygienic conditions. (Olukanni, 2013).  With research, reusable pads are said to be hygienic and economical. The reusable pads consist of two parts: a holder and a pad. The holder is made from durable tetron material, inside of which there is water proof bambino. A pad is made from cotton fabric with 2 layers of towel inside. Every pack consists of two holders and three pads, which will then be secured by a ribbon on two sides of the holder.
The product has been tested by several women and they were very satisfied with its quality. According to these test results, the cotton feels soft and the pad is easy to use and also to wash.  

Problem Analysis

The communities we are looking at are rural communities in Nigeria, with thousands of families, these villagers are mostly farmers and owners of petty stores whose income cannot feed their family let alone buy commercial disposable sanitary pads. From findings there are usually leakage from poor-quality protection materials and vaginal infections; inadequate waste disposal facilities, Absenteeism where girls stay at home rather than attend school when menstruating, Unhygienic ways to dry menstrual materials, Limited education about the facts of menstruation; Limited access to counseling and guidance; The lack of resources for washing such as soap and the unsupportive attitudes of some men. Therefore, the communities of these villages highly require support with MHM and WASH practice lecture and on the usage of Eco Reusable Pads.

Justification

Female students and women face great challenges in terms of hygiene and sanitation. For females who are menstruating, these problems compound the difficulties posed by the inability to afford sanitary towels as well as cultural taboos around menstruation.
Some local communities’ elders and women have requested participation from us to take the task of supporting Menstrual Hygiene Management (MHM) and Water, Sanitation and hygiene education (WASH) with health practitioners through development programs and follow up.

Goal

The objective of this project is to ensure that Girls and Women living in rural communities in Nigeria are educated to the very poor, disadvantaged village community to encourage communities on the importance of Menstrual Hygiene Management (MHM) and best Water, Hygiene and Sanitation (WASH) practices, with this Girls will not have to miss school while Women will not miss out on social active participation in their communities simply because they are menstruating. Advocates on Female Gender Mutilation (FGM), Gender Base Violence (GBV) and Health Workers will enlighten the women and girls on health related issues and lifestyles. Other objectives are; Distribution of 4,000 reusable pads and 1,000 laundry soap.  To improve school attendance among girls between the ages of 10 and 17 years in primary & secondary schools. To increase access to vulnerable adolescent girls and women with sanitary towels and comprehensive Menstrual Hygiene Management (MHM) knowledge, the importance of Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH) and proper disposal used sanitary towels as regards climate change.   To increase the self-esteem of needy girls between ages 10 to 17 years.   To improve economic status for women between 18-40 years. To document lessons learned and scale-up options documented.   To equip students both male and female and schools the required standards MHM and WASH. Willingness to recruit boys and girls in equal numbers to educate others in their communities and schools.  Entrepreneurship classes on Reusable Pad for the girls and women for sustainability and to make extra income by reselling and laundry soap training. And lectures and interactive sessions on the dangers, myths and truths about Gender Base Violence, Female Gender Mutilation and other female health related issues.

Activities

  • Preliminary visit.
  • Training women on how to make reusable pads and laundry soap.
  • Distribution of reusable pads and laundry soap.
  • Training on effective Menstrual Hygiene Management and best WASH practices.
  • Adequate knowledge of Gender Base Violence and Female Gender Mutilation from advocates and health workers.
